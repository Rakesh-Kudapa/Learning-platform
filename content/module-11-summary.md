# Module 11 — Summary

## Eyebrow / kicker
`MODULE 11 • Summary`

## Headline + lead
**Headline:** Putting it all *together*
**Lead:** Ten modules, one connected story: how everyday healthcare data
becomes the evidence that shapes real medical decisions. Here's the whole
arc in one place.

## Narration script
Let's bring it all together. Real-world data is what gets generated during
everyday care; real-world evidence is what you get after carefully
analysing it. That evidence spans the entire life sciences journey, but
it's strongest after a medicine reaches real patients. It exists because
clinical trials, however rigorous, are narrow, expensive, and end at
approval — real life needs answers trials can't give. Getting there means
moving data through collection, standardization, curation, and analysis,
guided by shared standards like OMOP and FHIR, built on real technology
infrastructure, and watched over by a wide circle of stakeholders. None of
it is easy — data quality, bias, and scale remain real challenges — but
modern data engineering and AI are making the work faster and more
consistent every year.

## Body sections

### Key takeaways
- **RWD is the ingredient, RWE is the meal** — collected data versus the
  conclusions drawn from analysing it.
- RWE spans the **whole life sciences lifecycle**, strongest after launch,
  but increasingly used earlier too.
- It exists because **trials have real limits**: narrow populations, high
  cost, short follow-up, artificial conditions.
- Getting from raw data to evidence takes a **deliberate six-stage
  process**: collect → ingest → standardize → curate/QC → analyse →
  evidence.
- A **wide circle of stakeholders** — inside and outside the company —
  both shapes and consumes that evidence.
- **Shared standards** (OMOP, FHIR, HL7, ICD-10, SNOMED, LOINC) are what
  make data from different places actually comparable.
- Real **technology infrastructure** (cloud, big-data tools, analytics
  languages) is what makes the work possible at scale.
- **Real challenges remain**: data quality, interoperability, privacy,
  bias, regulatory acceptance, and scale.
- **AI and data engineering** are accelerating this work — faster, more
  consistent, more scalable — without replacing careful human judgment.

## Knowledge checks

### Question 1
- **Question:** Which of these best summarizes why real-world evidence
  exists as a field?
- **Options:**
  A. To replace clinical trials entirely
  B. To answer questions trials can't affordably answer — about diverse,
     real patients, over the long term
  C. To avoid the need for data standards
  D. To eliminate the role of regulators
- **Correct answer:** B
- **Explanation:** As covered in Module 2, RWE doesn't replace trials — it
  fills the gaps trials structurally can't: narrow populations, high cost,
  short follow-up, and artificial conditions.
- **XP:** 15

  ingredient, evidence is the ___."

- **Correct answer:** A
