# Module 5 — Stakeholders

## Eyebrow / kicker
`MODULE 05 • Stakeholders`

## Headline + lead
**Headline:** Who's actually in the *room*
**Lead:** Real-world evidence isn't built by one team in isolation — it
passes through many hands, each with a different stake in getting it
right. Knowing who's who tells you who you're really working for.

## Narration script
Real-world evidence touches a surprisingly wide circle of people. Inside a
pharma or biotech company, RWE teams, market access specialists, medical
affairs, and drug safety teams all use it differently. Outside the company,
regulators decide whether it's strong enough to support an approval, payers
decide whether it justifies covering the cost, and doctors use it to guide
real treatment decisions. Data providers supply the raw material, and
behind it all are patients — the people the data comes from, and ultimately
the people all of this is meant to help.

## Body sections

### Inside the pharma or biotech company
- **RWE / Epidemiology team** — designs the studies, chooses data sources,
  and runs the analysis.
- **HEOR & Market Access** — uses RWE to build the value story that
  convinces payers to cover a treatment.
- **Medical Affairs** — uses RWE to answer clinician questions and support
  scientific communication after launch.
- **Drug Safety / Pharmacovigilance** — monitors real-world data for safety
  signals that wouldn't show up in a smaller trial.
- **Regulatory Affairs** — packages RWE for submission to agencies like the
  FDA or EMA.

### Outside the company
- **Data providers** — EHR vendors, claims aggregators, and registry holders
  who supply the raw real-world data. Specialist partners (like Excelra)
  curate and structure that data so it's actually usable.
- **Regulators** (FDA, EMA, and others) — decide how much weight RWE can
  carry in an approval or label decision.
- **Payers & HTA bodies** — insurers and health technology assessment
  groups (like NICE in the UK or ICER in the US) who decide what gets
  funded, based partly on real-world value evidence.
- **Healthcare providers** — doctors and hospitals who both generate the
  underlying data (through routine care) and later use RWE-backed
  guidance to treat patients.
- **Patients** — the original source of every data point, and the people
  every other stakeholder is ultimately trying to help.

### Who actually consumes the output
| Stakeholder | What they do with the evidence |
|---|---|
| Regulators | Decide whether to approve a drug or expand its label |
| Payers | Decide whether and how much to pay for a treatment |
| Clinicians | Decide how to treat an individual patient |
| Commercial teams | Decide strategy — pricing, positioning, market focus |

## Knowledge checks

### Question 1
- **Question:** Which stakeholder primarily decides whether an insurer
  will pay for a treatment?
- **Options:**
  A. Drug Safety team
  B. Payers / HTA bodies
  C. Patients
  D. Data providers
- **Correct answer:** B
- **Explanation:** Payers and Health Technology Assessment (HTA) bodies
  evaluate a treatment's value — often using RWE — to decide coverage and
  reimbursement. Drug Safety focuses on risk monitoring, not payment.
- **XP:** 15

### Question 2
- **Question:** Which internal team is primarily responsible for watching
  real-world data for rare safety signals?
- **Options:**
  A. Drug Safety / Pharmacovigilance
  B. Market Access
  C. Commercial / Strategy
  D. Regulatory Affairs
- **Correct answer:** A
- **Explanation:** Drug Safety / Pharmacovigilance teams monitor real-world
  data specifically to catch safety signals that a smaller trial wouldn't
  reveal.
- **XP:** 15

### Question 3
- **Question:** Who are both the original source of the data AND the
  ultimate intended beneficiary of real-world evidence?
- **Options:**
  A. Regulators
  B. Payers
  C. Patients
  D. Data providers
- **Correct answer:** C
- **Explanation:** Patients generate the underlying data through their
  care, and they're the people every other stakeholder is ultimately
  trying to help.
- **XP:** 15
